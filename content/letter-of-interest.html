Letter of Interest

Thank you for your interest in the Open Access Button. Signing a letter of interest helps us demonstrate formal support for our mission to funders, other libraries, and most importantly... ourselves!

Please ensure the letter is signed on behalf of a relavent department (e.g document delivery), or the whole library, with the latter being preferable. Upon signing, we'll send a confirmation email to those mentioned to verify the signing.

Dear SPARC,

I’m writing to express my deep interest in and support for the Open Access Button, a suite of tools enabling fast, easy discovery and requesting of scholarly outputs. In particular, we support the Open Access Button developing high-quality services for our library aimed at:

  • improving the usability (for patrons and staff), cost-effectiveness, and efficacy of “just in-time” (e.g Interlibrary Loan & purchase-on-demand) services,
  • strengthening libraries big deal negotiation positions and lowering requirements for journal subscriptions,
    • adapting existing service paradigms, such as Interlibrary Loan, for use in facilitating data and code sharing, and content mining,
  • increasing the use, reuse, and creation of Open content,
  • enabling investments in campus-level library services to serve broader communities and more strongly align with missions to democratize access to knowledge.

This project has the potential to create an important piece of infrastructure for libraries— one that is owned by the library community and not at risk of enclosure by a for-profit vendor. These services could help reduce library’s costs while promoting an open scholarly infrastructure and allowing libraries to better serve their users.

Our library would be happy to provide the Open Access Button team with feedback on functionality, consider piloting, and adoption of resulting services.
