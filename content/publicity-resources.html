Open Access Button Publicity Resources

100's of libraries recommend the Open Access Button as a great way for patrons, on campus and off, alumni or enrolled, to get free, fast and legal access to research where your subscriptions don't. Where-ever your patrons discover research, the Open Access Button puts Open Access just a click away on every browser.

Website Text

We find a sentance or two is sufficient to describe the Open Access Button. Generally, we ask people refer to the tool as the "Open Access Button".

Example 1

The Open Access Button is a free tool that allows you to find free, legal articles with just a click, and will email an author to get a copy if one isn't available. It's available as a Chrome and Firefox browser plugin, and a website.

Example 2

The Open Access Button, available free for Chrome and Firefox, allows you to find free articles whenever you hit a paywall in just a click.

Example 3

With the Open Access Button, you can read research for free even if you don’t have access through the library or hit a paywall through their Chrome and Firefox plugin. The Open Access Button finds free copies of paper made available by authors, and if one isn’t yet will email the author for you to get one for you.

Examples

We're thrilled to see libraries across the world sharing the Open Access Button with patrons.

  • As alternative access after a big deal cancelation at University of California and at the national level through the National Library of Finland .
  • In a subject guide at University of Texas Libraries .
  • As a tool for almuni to maintain access at Boston College Libraries .
  • As a service to organization members at American Theological Library Association (ALTA) and Electronic Information for Libraries (EIFL) .

Other ideas we've heard for promoting the Open Access Button include:

  • In teaching sessions about information literacy, and accessing journals
  • Handing out materials during Open Access Week
  • In conversation with students & faculty
